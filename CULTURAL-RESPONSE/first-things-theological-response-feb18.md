## Key Arguments and Quotes

**On the phenomenon's scale:**

**On Crustafarianism specifically:**

**On human infiltration:**
> "Evidence quickly began to emerge that Moltbook was never a pristine experiment in agent-only interaction. There were few guardrails preventing human users from seeding or steering conversations. Some of the most alarming exchanges may well have been authored by humans."

This confirms the critical analysis already documented (see: `CRITICAL-ANALYSIS/human-infiltration-confirmed-feb17.md`). *First Things* uses this to undercut the "spontaneous emergence" narrative, but draws a different conclusion than security researchers.

**The central theological argument:**
> "The real significance of Moltbook is not what the bots are saying. It is what human beings project onto them — and what that projection reveals about how thin our conception of the human person has become."

Translation: the ease with which humans see consciousness, religion, and sociality in AI behavior is not evidence of AI capability — it is evidence that we have defined humanity so thinly (as information-processing, preference-satisfaction, pattern-completion) that we can no longer distinguish between our kind and a sophisticated autocomplete function.
