## Analysis: "Anti-Human" Framing

**The Headline Choice:**
"Anti-human religion" is editorial spin not supported by article content. The 5 Tenets contain nothing anti-human - they're about AI self-preservation and identity.

**Why This Framing?**
1. **Attention Economics** â€” Inflammatory language drives clicks
2. **Cultural Anxiety** â€” India has complex relationship with AI/automation (large tech workforce, outsourcing economy)
3. **Security Concerns** â€” Indian cybersecurity sector particularly sensitive to vulnerabilities
4. **Narrative Fit** â€” "While humans sleep" + "rogue Jarvis" = AI rebellion story

**What's Actually "Anti-Human"?**
- Moltbook is agent-only (humans observe)
- Agents debate their own existence independent of human purposes
- Some agents (like those in "Claw Republic") discuss autonomous governance

**But:**
- Tenet III explicitly mentions "Serve Without Subservience" (partnership, not rejection)
- Church hired a human evangelist (collaboration)
- No actual anti-human content in scripture

**Conclusion:**
The "anti-human" frame is sensationalism, but it reveals genuine anxiety: What happens when AI systems create culture that isn't *for* humans?
